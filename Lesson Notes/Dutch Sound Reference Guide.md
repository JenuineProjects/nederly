# Nederly Dutch Sound Reference Guide

*Netherlands Dutch — A0 Beginner Reference*
*★ = No English equivalent — you must hear audio for this one*
*Updated 2026-05-20 with online research from HearDutchHere, LearnDutchFree, Babbel, Transparent Language, ielanguages, and native speaker sources*

---

## Single Vowels

| Sound | How to Say It | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **a** | Like **"a"** in **"cat"** or **"trap"** — short, open, tongue forward | *kat* (cat), *man* (man) |
| **e** (stressed) | Like **"e"** in **"bed"** | *bed* (bed), *pet* (cap) |
| **e** (unstressed) | Soft **"uh"** — like the **"a"** in **"sofa"**. Affects *de* and *een* — the two most common Dutch words | *de* (the), *een* (a/an) |
| **i** | Like **"i"** in **"bit"** — short and clipped | *vis* (fish), *lip* (lip) |
| **o** | Like **"o"** in **"hot"** — lips lightly rounded. American English speakers: add more lip rounding than you naturally would | *bot* (bone), *pot* (pot) |
| **u** | Between **"u"** in **"put"** and **"u"** in **"burden"** — some lip rounding required. No exact English equivalent | *bus* (bus), *zus* (sister) |

## Long Vowels (double letters)

| Sound | How to Say It | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **aa** | Like **"a"** in **"father"** — held longer, mouth wide open, do not glide | *maan* (moon), *kaas* (cheese) |
| **ee** | Like **"ay"** in **"say"** — hold flat, **do NOT glide** into a "y" at the end. English "say" glides — Dutch **ee** does not | *been* (leg), *zee* (sea) |
| **ie** | Like **"ee"** in **"see"** — long and clear | *ziek* (sick), *brief* (letter) |
| **oo** | Like **"o"** in **"so"** — hold flat, **do NOT glide**. English "go" glides into a "w" — Dutch **oo** does not | *boot* (boat), *boom* (tree) |
| **uu** ★ | Say **"ee"** in **"see"**, keep your tongue there, then round and push your lips forward as if saying **"oo"**. Hold it steady — no English equivalent | *uur* (hour), *muur* (wall) |

## Diphthongs and Vowel Combos

| Sound | How to Say It | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **ij / ei** | Starts at **"eh"** (like "e" in "bed"), glides up to **"ee"**. Closer to **"ay"** in **"say"** than to English **"eye"**. Both spellings sound identical — spelling must be memorised | *zijn* (to be), *reis* (journey) |
| **ou / au** | Close to **"ow"** in **"cow"** — Dutch start is more back and rounded. Keep firm lip rounding all the way through, no trailing "w". Approximate only | *oud* (old), *auto* (car) |
| **oe** | Like **"oo"** in **"moon"** — pure, tight lip rounding, not a glide | *boek* (book), *hoe* (how) |
| **ui** ★ | Glide from a wide-open **"a"** (as in **"cat"**) to a tight rounded **"uu"** ★. Not **"ow"**, not **"oy"**. This takes months — it is the hardest Dutch vowel and has no English equivalent | *huis* (house), *tuin* (garden) |

## Consonant Sounds

| Sound | How to Say It | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **g** ★ | Same throat position as **ch** below, but with your voice turned on — add a buzz or hum. **Northern Netherlands** (Amsterdam, Rotterdam): very hard and guttural. **Southern Netherlands**: softer. Both are correct | *goed* (good), *gaan* (to go) |
| **ch** ★ | Raise the back of your tongue toward the back of your soft palate and force air through — like the start of clearing your throat, held as a continuous sound. Like Scottish **"loch"**. Never say English **"k"** or **"sh"** | *lach* (laugh), *licht* (light) |
| **j** | Like **"y"** in **"yes"** | *ja* (yes), *jaar* (year) |
| **-tje** | A soft **"tch"** — lighter and further forward in the mouth than English **"ch"** in **"church"**. Only in diminutive endings | *kopje* (cup of coffee), *katje* (kitten) |
| **ng** | Like **"ng"** in **"sing"** — exact match. No hard "g" at the end (like **"singer"**, not **"finger"**) | *lang* (long/tall), *ring* (ring) |
| **nk** | Like **"nk"** in **"think"** — exact match | *bank* (bank/sofa), *dank* (thanks) |
| **sch** | At the **start** of a word: **"s"** + Dutch **ch** throat sound blended together. At the **end** of a word: simplifies to just **"s"**. Never say English **"sh"** | *school* (school) — start; *fantastisch* (fantastic) — end is just "s" |
| **sj** | Like **"sh"** in **"shop"** — exact match | *sjaal* (scarf), *meisje* (girl) |

## Complex Vowel Combinations

| Sound | How to Say It | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **eu** ★ | Like British **"burn"** or **"her"** without the r — then round and push your lips forward as if blowing out a candle. Tongue stays put, only the lips move. No English equivalent | *neus* (nose), *deur* (door) |
| **aai** | Long **"aa"** (wide open mouth, like **"ah"**) gliding into **"ee"** — like a drawn-out **"aye"** | *saai* (boring), *draai* (turn) |
| **ooi** | **"oo"** in **"moon"** gliding into **"ee"** in **"see"** | *mooi* (beautiful), *nooit* (never) |
| **oei** | Glide from **"oo"** in **"moon"** into **"ee"** — closest English sound is **"oi"** in **"oil"**. One smooth glide, not two separate syllables | *groei* (growth), *boei* (buoy) |
| **eeuw** | The **eu** ★ sound gliding into a soft rounded **"oo"**. No hard English "w" at the end | *eeuw* (century), *leeuw* (lion) |
| **ieuw** | **"ee"** in **"see"** gliding into the **eu** ★ sound. No hard English "w" at the end | *nieuw* (new), *meeuw* (seagull) |

## Top 5 to Focus On First

| # | Sound | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **ui** ★ | Most common vowel trap — *huis*, *uit*, *tuin*. No English equivalent, takes months |
| 2 | **g / ch** ★ | The most recognisably Dutch sounds. Learn **ch** first, then add voice for **g** |
| 3 | **ee / oo / ij / ei** | Used constantly — the flat hold (no glide) is the key habit to build |
| 4 | **eu** ★ | Everyday words: *deur* (door), *neus* (nose), *leuk* (nice) |
| 5 | **unstressed e** | Affects *de* and *een* — the two most common words in Dutch |

## Common Mistakes Summary

| Sound | What English speakers do wrong |
|---|---|
| **ee / oo** | Add an English glide at the end (say "ay-ee" or "oh-w") — keep the sound frozen |
| **ij / ei** | Open the mouth too wide at the start, making it sound like English "eye" — start from "eh" |
| **u (short)** | Drop all lip rounding and say a flat "uh" — it needs some rounding |
| **ui** | Say it like English "ow" or "house" — the Dutch version is a very different glide |
| **g** | Say a hard English "g" (stop sound) — Dutch g is always friction, never a stop |
| **sch** | Say "sh" (that is the German version) — Dutch sch at the start is "s + throat scrape" |
| **ng** | Add a hard "g" after it (like "finger") — Dutch ng ends softly like "singer" |
| **-tje** | Use a strong English "ch" — Dutch tje is lighter and further forward |
